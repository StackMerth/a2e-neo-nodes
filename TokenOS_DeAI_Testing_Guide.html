Before you start

What you're testing

A batch of new and recently shipped features across the TokenOS platform: the public marketplace homepage, a new credit balance system, email verification, real-time connection indicator, install-to-home-screen support, a new GPU type, mobile sidebar behavior, and some redesigned dashboards.

What you need

  • The credentials in the orange box above
  • A laptop or desktop computer (Chrome, Edge, or Safari) for sections 1–5 and 9
  • A smartphone (iOS Safari or Android Chrome) for sections 6, 7, 8
  • A WiFi connection you can switch off and on for section 7

How to report problems

Take a screenshot of anything that looks wrong, blank, or unexpected. Note the exact step number you were on (e.g. "Section 3.5"). Send everything back in one message.

Tip: Each section can be done independently. If you only have time for a few, prioritize Sections 1, 3, and 4 — those cover the highest-impact features.
1

Marketplace homepage

5 minutes · no login required

Open https://market.tokenos.ai/ in your browser.

Step 1.1
Find the "Bring your tools" trust strip

Scroll past the big hero. You will find a section with:

  • An eyebrow label: The stack you already run
  • A two-line display headline: "Bring your tools. Run them on real silicon."
  • A 3-number stat band: 10+ Frameworks & tools / 100% NVIDIA inventory / Open Source on GitHub
  • A bento layout: a large NVIDIA hero tile on the left with a "Featured" eyebrow and description, plus 9 smaller satellite tiles (CUDA, PyTorch, TensorFlow, HuggingFace, vLLM, Ollama, Docker, Ubuntu, GitHub)
  • A small footnote at the bottom about not representing the listed projects

On desktop: hover slowly over each satellite tile. You should see a thin brand-colored line slide in along the bottom, the wordmark nudge slightly right, the colored dot grow slightly, and a very subtle color wash appear. Hover the NVIDIA tile: the green accent bar at the top should expand from one-third to full width.

Pass: bento layout renders correctly, all hover effects work, NVIDIA hero tile is visually dominant.
Step 1.2
Mobile-width test

Drag the browser window narrower until it is roughly phone-sized.

Pass: the NVIDIA tile stacks at the top full-width, the other 9 tiles form a 2-column grid below, nothing overlaps.
Step 1.3
Cost calculator section

Keep scrolling. You will find "What you would pay elsewhere".

Left side: a GPU tier picker (6 buttons), a GPU count box with + and − buttons, a slider with three preset buttons.

Right side: a big green dollar amount, a percent-off message, and four price tiles (TokenOS, AWS, Azure, GCP).

Try this sequence:

  1. Click H100 → TokenOS price and three hyperscaler prices appear, TokenOS is far cheaper.
  2. Click + next to GPU count a few times → all numbers scale up.
  3. Click the Light use preset → numbers shrink.
  4. Click L40S → numbers refresh.
  5. Click B200 → the right side changes to "NVIDIA B200 is not generally available on AWS, Azure, or GCP today." with only the TokenOS price. No invented hyperscaler numbers.
  6. Click B300 and GB300 → same honest "not on hyperscalers" message.
  7. Click back to H100 → comparison view returns.
Pass: numbers update smoothly on every click, B-series tiers honestly admit no hyperscaler equivalent exists.
2

Sign in & email verification

5 minutes

Go to https://user.tokenos.ai/ and sign in with the credentials in the orange box above.

Step 2.1
Look for the verification banner

Right after signing in, look at the top of the dashboard.

  • If you see a yellow banner saying "Verify your email" with a "Resend email" button: continue to step 2.2.
  • If you do not see a banner: this account is already verified. Skip to step 2.3.
Step 2.2
Trigger and complete email verification

Click the Resend email button on the banner. A green confirmation appears.

Open the inbox listed in the orange credentials box. You should receive an email within 30 seconds. It should have:

  • Subject: "Verify your email — TokenOS_DeAI"
  • Header showing the two-tone wordmark: white "TokenOS" + green "_DeAI"
  • A "Verify email" button

Click the button. You should land on a "verified successfully" page.

Go back to the portal tab and refresh. The yellow banner should be gone, a green notification briefly pops up, and the bell icon top-right shows a new notification.

Pass: email arrives with TokenOS_DeAI branding, click verifies the account, banner disappears, notification appears.
Step 2.3
Connection status indicator

Look at the top-right header area, just to the left of the bell icon. On first page load you may briefly see a small icon (green WiFi or a spinner). After about 5 seconds it should fade away.

Pass: icon appears briefly on load, then auto-hides.
3

Balance & topup flow — main test

15 minutes
Step 3.1
Find the Balance page

In the left sidebar, look for "Balance" with a wallet icon (sits between "Active Compute" and "Billing"). Click it.

You should see:

  • Page title "Balance" with a green wallet icon
  • A big $0.00 balance (if you have never topped up)
  • Three small stat boxes: Lifetime topped up / Lifetime spent / Refunded back
  • A "Transactions" panel saying "No transactions yet"
Pass: page loads cleanly, no error.
Step 3.2
Open the Top up dialog

Click the green Top up button (top right).

A pop-up appears with:

  • Title "Top up balance" and a one-line subtitle about sending USDC
  • A box showing "SEND USDC ON SOLANA DEVNET TO" followed by a long Solana wallet address and a small copy icon
  • Three labeled inputs: Transaction hash, USD amount sent, Note (optional)
  • Cancel and green "Credit balance" buttons at the bottom
Pass: modal opens with this layout, no broken elements.
Step 3.3
Test the copy button

Click the copy icon next to the wallet address.

Pass: the icon briefly changes to a green checkmark for about 1.5 seconds.
Step 3.4
Credit a test balance

In the Transaction hash field, type or paste exactly the test transaction code from the orange box at the top of this document:

DEV_test_topup_001

(This is a special test code that works only on the test version of the site. Real customers would paste their real Solana transaction.)

In the USD amount sent field, type 500.

In the Note field, optionally type something like "Tester first topup".

Click Credit balance.

The button briefly shows a spinning loader ("Verifying…"), then:

  • Green notification at the top: "Credited $500.00 to your balance."
  • Modal closes, Balance page refreshes
Pass: balance now shows $500.00. "Lifetime topped up" shows $500. Transactions list has one green entry: +$500.00 with description "Solana topup".
Fail: if you see a red error like "invalid_body" or "Topup failed", screenshot it and stop.
Step 3.5
Duplicate-protection test (this is intentional)

Click Top up again. Paste the same code (DEV_test_topup_001), amount 500. Submit.

Pass: notification says "This transaction was already credited." Balance stays at $500 (does NOT become $1000). Transactions list still has only one +$500 entry.
Step 3.6
Top up a second time with a fresh code

Open the dialog again. Use a different code:

DEV_test_topup_002

Set amount to 100. Submit.

Pass: notification "Credited $100.00". Balance now $600.00. Two green transactions visible.
Step 3.7
Spend the balance on a rental

Click Request Compute in the sidebar.

Fill out the form:

  • GPU tier: click H100
  • Number of GPUs: leave at 1
  • Duration: leave at default (1 or 7 days)
  • Workload type: leave at default

Scroll down. Cost summary shows roughly $140 for 1 day (or higher for longer durations).

Step 3.8
Find the new payment method picker

Scroll to find a section called "Payment method". It should have 2 or 3 tiles side by side:

  • USDC on Solana (green)
  • Pre-loaded balance (cyan, showing "Available: $600.00")
  • Possibly Operator balance (blue)
Pass: the cyan "Pre-loaded balance" tile is present and shows $600.
Step 3.9
Click "Pre-loaded balance"

The cyan tile should highlight. Scroll further to the Payment section at the bottom. It should now show a math panel:

  • Balance before: $600.00
  • This rental: −$140.15 (or similar)
  • Balance after: $459.85

The big "Transaction Hash (Solana)" paste field should be hidden (only shows for the USDC option).

Pass: math is correct, paste field is hidden.
Step 3.10
Submit the rental

If the form requires an SSH public key, paste this dummy value:

ssh-rsa AAAAB3NzaC1yc2EAAAADAQABAAABAQDQ test@tester

Click Submit Request at the bottom. A success notification appears, then the page navigates to a rental details page.

Pass: rental is created successfully.
Step 3.11
Confirm the balance was debited

Click Balance in the sidebar.

Pass:
  • Balance now shows $459.85 (600 minus the rental cost)
  • A new RED entry at the top of the Transactions list: −$140.15 with description "Rental 1x H100 for 1d"
  • Three transactions total visible (two green topups, one red rental)
Step 3.12
Insufficient-balance warning

Go back to Request Compute. Set Number of GPUs to 5, Duration to 30 days. Total cost is now way over $459.85.

Stay on "Pre-loaded balance" as the payment method.

Pass:
  • A red warning under the picker: "Need $X but only $459.85 in your balance. Top up at Balance or switch to USDC."
  • The Submit Request button is greyed out
Step 3.13
Regression check: USDC still works

Click the USDC on Solana tile.

Pass: the "Transaction Hash (Solana)" paste field reappears. Submit button is no longer disabled.

New L40S GPU type

3 minutes

The platform just added a new GPU type called L40S. It should appear everywhere GPU options are listed.

Step 4.1
Request Compute page

Go to Request Compute in the sidebar. The GPU tier grid should now include an L40S card with a price tag of around $0.88/hr (about $21/day). Color accent is cyan/teal.

Pass: L40S card is present.
Step 4.2
Workload-type compatibility

Click the L40S card to select it. Try each of the three Workload Type options (Inference / Training / Mixed) in turn.

Pass: L40S stays selectable for all three workload types. The three "consumer" cards at the bottom (RTX 4090 / RTX 3090 / Consumer) correctly grey out for Training and Mixed.
Step 4.3
Deploy a Node page

If the test account has node-runner access, look for a "Deploy" link in the sidebar. The GPU tier picker there should also include an L40S option.

Pass: L40S appears there too.
5

Dashboard polish

3 minutes · may be empty for fresh accounts
Step 5.1
Operator dashboard polish

If the test account is a node-runner, navigate to the main dashboard.

Look for the Earnings Forecast card. It should be visually polished with:

  • A headline projection (like "$X projected next 30 days")
  • A small sparkline (mini chart showing trend)
  • A trend indicator chip (up/down arrow with percentage)
  • A range bar showing low–high estimate

Look for the Earnings, last 30 days chart. If the account has earnings history, you should see gradient bars. If brand new, this will be empty — that is fine, skip.

Pass: forecast card looks polished, chart renders correctly (or is empty for new accounts).
Step 5.2
Deploy a Node header (node-runner side)

Go to the Deploy page. The header at the top should be a properly padded card with:

  • A green rocket icon
  • "Deploy a Node" title
  • A subtitle about selecting GPU tier
  • A subtle green-tinted border around the whole card with breathing room on all sides

The green tint should NOT hug the text tightly. There should be visible padding inside the card.

Pass: header card has visible padding and breathing room.

Mobile sidebar auto-collapse

3 minutes · phone required

On your phone, open https://user.tokenos.ai/ and sign in (if not already).

Step 6.1
Buyer sidebar collapse-on-click (mobile only)

You are testing the BUYER side specifically. Make sure you are on a buyer page (e.g., /buyer/dashboard).

Tap the menu icon (☰) at the top-left to open the sidebar. The sidebar slides out.

Now tap any menu item, for example "Request Compute".

Pass: the page navigates AND the sidebar closes automatically. You should not have to tap elsewhere to close it.
Step 6.2
Desktop regression check

On your laptop, do the same: click any sidebar menu item.

Pass: on desktop, the sidebar does NOT auto-close. This behavior is mobile-only.
7

Install to Home Screen

5 minutes · phone required

The portal can now be installed like a real app.

Step 7.1
Android Chrome

On Android, open https://user.tokenos.ai/ in Chrome.

After a few seconds you should see an "Install app" prompt at the bottom of the screen, or in the three-dot menu under "Install app" / "Add to Home Screen".

Tap to install. A "TokenOS" icon appears on your home screen. Tap it.

Pass: the portal opens WITHOUT the Chrome address bar — it looks like a standalone app.
Step 7.2
iOS Safari

On iPhone, open https://user.tokenos.ai/ in Safari.

Tap the Share button (square with arrow up) at the bottom. Scroll down the share sheet and tap "Add to Home Screen".

A "TokenOS" icon appears on your home screen. Tap it.

Pass: opens in standalone mode (no Safari address bar). May need to sign in again the first time — that is normal for iOS.
8

Real-time connection indicator

5 minutes · WiFi toggle required

Back to laptop/desktop. Open https://user.tokenos.ai/.

Step 8.1
Normal state

When you first load the page, look at the top-right header area, just left of the bell icon. You should briefly see a small icon (likely green WiFi or a spinner) that fades away after about 5 seconds.

Step 8.2
Disconnect WiFi

While the page is open, turn off your laptop's WiFi.

Within a few seconds, an icon should appear in that same header spot:

  • First: a yellow spinning icon (the platform is trying to reconnect)
  • After a few more seconds: a red WiFi-off icon (the platform gave up, you are offline)

Hover your mouse over the icon. A tooltip should explain what is happening.

Pass: icon appears, color transitions from yellow to red, tooltip is informative.
Step 8.3
Reconnect

Turn WiFi back on.

Within a few seconds the icon should switch to:

  • Briefly yellow spinning (trying to reconnect)
  • Then green pulsing WiFi (connected)
  • Then fades away after 5 seconds
Pass: full cycle works automatically without needing to refresh the page.
